# Delivery Exception Procedure

A replacement can be considered when:
- The order has a carrier exception or no recovery scan.
- The package has had no meaningful scan for at least 48 hours.
- The estimated delivery is missing or outside the promised or customer-needed window.
- The order does not require manual review.
- Replacement inventory is available.
- Carrier quote data is available for the replacement inventory location.

When handling a delayed order:
1. Identify the order.
2. Check tracking status.
3. Ask for the customer's deadline if it was not provided.
4. Check replacement inventory locations.
5. Quote shipping options for available replacement locations.
6. If the same item can arrive before the deadline, offer it first.
7. If the same item cannot arrive before the deadline but a substitute option can, explain the substitution and ask whether it is acceptable.
8. If no option can arrive before the deadline, offer a priority support case.
9. Do not describe expected delivery as guaranteed unless the tool marks it as guaranteed.
10. Confirm shipping address and customer approval before creating a replacement.
